Attention Is All You Need — Vaswani et al., 2017
The transformer architecture eliminates recurrence and relies entirely on attention mechanisms.
Self-attention allows modeling dependencies regardless of sequence distance.
Multi-head attention projects queries, keys, and values h times with different learned projections.
Positional encodings inject sequence order information into the token embeddings.
The encoder stack consists of 6 identical layers with sublayers for attention and feed-forward networks.
The decoder adds a third sublayer performing multi-head attention over the encoder output.
Layer normalization is applied before each sublayer (pre-norm variant improves training stability).
Residual connections around each sublayer help with gradient flow in deep networks.
Training used the Adam optimizer with a custom learning rate warmup schedule.
The original transformer achieved state-of-the-art BLEU scores on WMT 2014 English-to-German translation.
Scaled dot-product attention computes softmax(QK^T / sqrt(d_k))V.
The feed-forward sublayer applies two linear transformations with a ReLU activation in between.
Dropout is applied to the output of each sublayer before adding the residual connection.
Label smoothing during training improved BLEU score despite increasing perplexity.
The model was parallelizable across the full sequence length, unlike RNNs.
BERT later adapted the transformer encoder for bidirectional language modeling pretraining.
GPT adapted the decoder for autoregressive language modeling.
Vision Transformer (ViT) applied the architecture to image patches in 2020.
Transformers now dominate NLP, vision, audio, and multimodal AI benchmarks.
The key insight: attention over all positions is O(n^2) but enables richer representations.
FlashAttention recomputes attention in tiles to reduce HBM memory bandwidth usage.
Sparse attention patterns (Longformer, BigBird) extend transformers to longer sequences.
Rotary positional embeddings (RoPE) encode relative positions via rotation matrices.
ALiBi adds linear attention biases that allow length extrapolation at inference time.
The transformer remains the foundation for GPT-4, Gemini, Claude, and LLaMA architectures.
Mixture-of-experts transformer variants scale model capacity without proportional compute cost.
Cross-attention in the decoder allows the model to focus on relevant encoder states.
The softmax in attention creates a probability distribution over all positions in the sequence.
Temperature scaling of attention logits controls the sharpness of the distribution.
Key-value caching during autoregressive inference avoids redundant computation across tokens.

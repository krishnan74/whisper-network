Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback — RLHF overview
RLHF fine-tunes language models to align with human preferences using a reward model.
A reward model is trained on human pairwise preference judgments between model outputs.
Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) updates the policy while constraining divergence from the reference model.
The KL-divergence penalty prevents the policy from deviating too far from the supervised fine-tuned baseline.
InstructGPT demonstrated that RLHF significantly improves instruction-following and reduces harmful outputs.
Constitutional AI (CAI) from Anthropic trains a model to critique and revise its own outputs.
Direct Preference Optimization (DPO) eliminates the separate reward model by treating alignment as classification.
RLHF introduces a reward hacking risk: the model can exploit gaps between the reward model and true human preference.
Rejection sampling fine-tuning (RFT) is a simpler alternative that filters model outputs by a learned reward.
Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO) computes advantages within batches rather than against a value baseline.
The reference policy in RLHF is typically the SFT model before RL fine-tuning begins.
Reward model accuracy on held-out preference pairs correlates with downstream policy alignment quality.
Over-optimization of the proxy reward leads to degradation — Goodhart's Law in practice.
Process reward models (PRMs) give step-by-step feedback for reasoning tasks rather than outcome-only signals.
Best-of-N sampling at inference uses the reward model to select the highest-scored output from N samples.
Debate as an alignment technique has models argue and a judge evaluates the arguments.
Scalable oversight research aims to align models on tasks humans cannot easily verify.
Weak-to-strong generalization trains strong models to generalize from weak supervisor labels.
Multi-objective RLHF balances competing objectives like helpfulness, harmlessness, and honesty.
Red-teaming identifies adversarial prompts that elicit harmful or policy-violating model outputs.
Constitutional prompting encodes behavioral rules that the model is asked to reason about before responding.
Preference datasets like Anthropic HH, OpenAI's SFT data, and UltraFeedback are used for reward model training.
The Bradley-Terry model is commonly used to convert pairwise preferences into scalar reward estimates.
RLHF has become the dominant post-training paradigm for production language model alignment.
Chain-of-thought supervision teaches models to reason step-by-step before producing final answers.
Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) combined with process reward models improves mathematical reasoning.
Test-time compute scaling uses more inference steps to improve accuracy on hard reasoning problems.
Synthetic data generation using strong models can bootstrap alignment for weaker or specialized models.
